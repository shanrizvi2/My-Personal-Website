The MUN Experience

The first time I did Model United Nations (MUN), I was a shy sixth grade that looked half his age, and I was attending a conference I had heard about through a friend called EPMUN. Throughout the conference I mostly had no idea what was going on; the one time I summoned up the courage to say something I was made a fool of. It was clear that I did not belong there, but that feeling of humiliation struck a chord in me; and so I persisted.

I attended every local MUN conference I could, and by watching others and speaking at every opportunity, I improved -- immensely. By the time I traveled to Istanbul for my first international conference in eighth grade, I was ready. Initially, I was not taken seriously (I looked like a fifth grader in what was a high school conference), but I was used to that. Utilizing what I had learned, I became main submitter to my resolution, lobbied to make half of the over 100 delegates in my committee my signatories, and eventually won the award for best delegate. It was then when I realized that this was something that I enjoyed and excelled at.

When I moved to Dhahran High School, I demonstrated my abilities and quickly became the youngest officer ever on the DHSMUN team. I traveled to Berlin, New York and other cities as a delegate and chair, proving myself on the international stage. Throughout high school I continued to move up the ranks, moving from officer to junior executive, junior executive to senior executive, and now from senior executive to Secretary-General. I am confident that this year our conference will be the best it has ever been.
